The problem

YouTube was never built to remember your place.

Long playlists are where real learning happens — and where YouTube quietly lets you down.

You forget where you stopped

Close the tab, come back tomorrow, and the exact video — let alone the exact second — is gone. You're left guessing whether you watched half of lecture 12 or all of it, so you either rewatch material you already know or skip ahead and miss something important.

You search through hundreds of videos

Course playlists run 40, 100, even 200 videos deep. Scrolling to find "the one" wastes real time you could be spending actually learning — and it only gets worse the longer the playlist and the further into it you are.

Your timestamps disappear

YouTube's own "continue watching" is unreliable across playlists, devices, and sign-outs, and it doesn't understand playlists as a sequence at all. Sign out, switch devices, or watch something else in between, and your place in that 40-part course is the first thing to go.

Features

Everything you need to pick up exactly where you left off.

Built specifically around how long playlists actually get watched.

Resume Instantly

One click drops you back into the exact video and timestamp, no scrubbing required. You save the minutes you'd otherwise spend hunting for your spot, every single time you sit down to watch.

Auto Save

Your position saves silently in the background every few seconds. Nothing to click, nothing to lose — even if a tab crashes or you close the browser mid-video.

Track Playlists

Track any number of playlists at once and see progress across every one at a glance. It's the difference between juggling five open courses in your head and seeing them laid out in one place.

Tags

Label playlists as "Work," "Course," or "Music" so your library stays organized as it grows. Finding the right playlist becomes a filter, not a scroll.

Categories

Group playlists into categories like "Learning" or "Entertainment" to keep long lists scannable. Your dashboard stays useful even after you've tracked fifty playlists instead of five.

Colors

Assign a color to each playlist for instant recognition when you have dozens tracked. A quick glance tells you which is which before you even read the title.

Import / Export JSON

Your saved progress is yours — back it up or move it between machines with a single JSON file. No account or cloud dependency required to keep your data safe.

Dark Mode

An interface that's easy on the eyes during late-night study sessions or long watch marathons. It also matches the theme most people already watch YouTube in.

Light Mode

A crisp, bright interface for whenever you'd rather work in daylight. Switching takes one click, and your data looks exactly the same either way.

Under the hood

Native YouTube Integration

No separate app, no extra tab, no copy-pasting links. Playlist Progress Saver lives quietly inside YouTube itself, reading playlist and timestamp data directly from the page as you watch — so the experience feels like a part of YouTube rather than a tool bolted on top of it.

How it works

Four steps. Zero setup after that.

There's no configuration, no linking accounts, and nothing to remember to do. Track a playlist once and Playlist Progress Saver takes care of the rest.

01

Track a playlist

Open any YouTube playlist and click "Track" from the extension popup. It's a one-time action per playlist — no settings to configure.

02

Watch normally

Keep watching the way you already do — nothing changes about the YouTube player, the layout, or how videos play.

03

Progress saves automatically

Every few seconds, your current video and timestamp are quietly stored in the background, with no prompts or pop-ups to interrupt you.

04

Resume instantly

Open the popup any time and jump straight back to the exact second you left, on this device or wherever you track it again.
