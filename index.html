The problem

YouTube was never built to remember your place.

Long playlists are where real learning happens — and where YouTube quietly lets you down.

You forget where you stopped

Close the tab, come back tomorrow, and the exact video — let alone the exact second — is gone.

You search through hundreds of videos

Course playlists run 40, 100, even 200 videos deep. Scrolling to find "the one" wastes real time.

Your timestamps disappear

YouTube's own "continue watching" is unreliable across playlists, devices, and sign-outs.

Under the hood

Native YouTube Integration

How it works

Four steps. Zero setup after that.

01

Track a playlist

Open any YouTube playlist and click "Track" from the extension popup.

02

Watch normally

Keep watching the way you already do — nothing changes about the YouTube player.

03

Progress saves automatically

Every few seconds, your current video and timestamp are quietly stored.

04

Resume instantly

Open the popup any time and jump straight back to the exact second you left.
